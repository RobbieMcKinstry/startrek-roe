Rules of Engagement

This page contains the Rules of Engagement (RoE) for Star Trek Fleet Command: Server 44. The Rules are indicated below. If the terminology in the rules is unfamilar to you, Coreplex's blog. provides a helpful glossary. All definitions of terms used in the Rules are as described on the Coreplex blog.

These rules were last updated on September 17, 2020.

    General

  1. If a ship or station is "protected", then attacking it is an act of war, except as noted under Retaliation, and therefore attacking a protected target is a violation of these rules.

    Survey Ships and Miners

  2. Survey ships are protected while UPC, unless they have participated in PvP combat or on a zero node, as indicated below.
  3. Survey ships that are OPC are not protected.
  4. Survey ships that are on a zero node are protected until a warning message has been sent to the ship ower. At which point, the ship is no longer protected.
  5. Survey ships that have recently participated in PvP combat are not protected. Examples of such unprotected combat include station defense, station raids. Clearing a zeroed mining node does not count as PvP combat for the purposes of this rule.

    6. Kill On Sight

  6. If your alliance has determined a player or enemy alliance to be "Kill on Sight" (KOS), they are not protected under any circumstances.
  7. Only alliance admirals can deem a player to be Kill on Sight.

    8. Retaliation

  8. If an aggressor attacks a protected target in violation of these rules, the victim may retaliate against the aggressor exactly once within 24 hours of the initial attack, despite any protected the aggressor may have.

    9. Warships and Stations

  9. Warships do not have protected status, except as noted under Events and Alliances
  10. Stations do not have protected status, except as noted under Events and Alliances

    11. Events

  11. During Scopely-official in-game events, systems in which events are occurring provide protected status to all UPC ships.
  12. Additionally, OPC ships are not protected, and may be attacked only after a warning is sent to the ower of the OPC ship.
  13. Swarm space (i.e. systems which only contain swarm hostiles) are considered Event space for the purposes of these rules.
